# Healthcare Guide: Mental Health and Common Conditions

## Depression

### Overview
Depression is a mental health disorder characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest in activities. It affects how a person thinks, feels, and functions.

### Types
- Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
- Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)
- Bipolar Disorder
- Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)
- Postpartum Depression
- Psychotic Depression

### Risk Factors
- Family history of depression
- Traumatic life events
- Chronic medical conditions
- Substance abuse
- Certain medications
- Stress
- Low self-esteem
- Social isolation

### Symptoms
- Persistent sad mood
- Loss of interest in activities
- Significant weight gain or loss
- Sleep disturbances (insomnia or oversleeping)
- Fatigue or low energy
- Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
- Difficulty concentrating
- Suicidal thoughts or self-harm urges

### Diagnosis
- Psychiatric evaluation
- Patient interview
- Depression screening tests (PHQ-9, Hamilton Scale)
- Medical tests to rule out other conditions

### Treatment Options
1. Psychotherapy:
   - Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
   - Interpersonal Therapy (IPT)
   - Psychodynamic Therapy
   - Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

2. Medications:
   - SSRIs (Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors): sertraline, fluoxetine, paroxetine
   - SNRIs (Serotonin-Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors): venlafaxine, duloxetine
   - Tricyclic Antidepressants
   - Atypical Antidepressants
   - Monoamine Oxidase Inhibitors (MAOIs)

3. Lifestyle Changes:
   - Regular exercise
   - Healthy sleep schedule
   - Social connection
   - Stress management
   - Limit alcohol and drugs
   - Healthy diet

### Complications
- Suicide risk
- Anxiety disorders
- Substance abuse
- Physical health problems
- Relationship difficulties
- Work or school problems


## Anxiety Disorders

### Overview
Anxiety disorders are characterized by excessive worry and fear that interferes with daily activities. They are among the most common mental health conditions.

### Types
- Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)
- Social Anxiety Disorder
- Panic Disorder
- Agoraphobia
- Specific Phobias
- Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)
- Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

### Risk Factors
- Family history of anxiety
- Traumatic experiences
- Chronic stress
- Medical conditions (thyroid problems, heart disease)
- Substance abuse
- Caffeine overuse
- Sleep problems

### Symptoms
- Excessive worry or fear
- Restlessness
- Difficulty concentrating
- Muscle tension
- Sleep disturbances
- Irritability
- Panic attacks
- Avoidance of triggering situations

### Treatment Options
1. Psychotherapy:
   - Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
   - Exposure Therapy
   - Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

2. Medications:
   - SSRIs: sertraline, paroxetine, escitalopram
   - SNRIs: venlafaxine, duloxetine
   - Benzodiazepines: lorazepam, alprazolam
   - Beta-blockers


## Asthma

### Overview
Asthma is a chronic inflammatory disorder of the airways characterized by reversible airflow obstruction, bronchial hyperresponsiveness, and inflammation.

### Symptoms
- Wheezing
- Shortness of breath
- Chest tightness
- Coughing (especially at night or during exercise)
- Fatigue

### Risk Factors
- Family history of asthma
- Allergies
- Respiratory infections in childhood
- Environmental exposure
- Obesity

### Treatment
1. Quick-Relief Medications:
   - Short-acting beta-2 agonists: albuterol

2. Long-Term Control:
   - Inhaled corticosteroids: fluticasone, budesonide
   - Long-acting beta-2 agonists
   - Combination inhalers
   - Leukotriene modifiers

### Management
- Keep asthma action plan
- Identify and avoid triggers
- Monitor with peak flow meter
- Take medications as prescribed
- Stay physically active
- Manage triggers like allergens, pollution, cold air


## High Cholesterol

### Overview
High cholesterol is a condition where there is too much cholesterol in the blood. It can increase the risk of heart disease and stroke.

### Risk Factors
- Unhealthy diet (high in saturated fats and trans fats)
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Obesity
- Age
- Family history
- Smoking
- Diabetes
- Certain medications

### Symptoms
High cholesterol often has no symptoms. It's detected through blood tests.

### Cholesterol Levels
- Total cholesterol: Less than 200 is desirable
- LDL cholesterol (bad): Less than 100 is optimal
- HDL cholesterol (good): 60 or higher is protective
- Triglycerides: Less than 150 is normal

### Treatment Options
1. Lifestyle Changes:
   - Reduce saturated fat intake
   - Eliminate trans fats
   - Eat more fiber (soluble fiber from oats, beans, apples)
   - Exercise regularly (150 minutes per week)
   - Maintain healthy weight
   - Don't smoke
   - Limit alcohol

2. Medications:
   - Statins (atorvastatin, simvastatin, rosuvastatin)
   - Bile acid sequestrants
   - Nicotinic acid
   - Fibrates
   - Ezetimibe
   - PCSK9 inhibitors

### Monitoring
- Get cholesterol levels checked regularly
- Monitor progress with medications
- Track lifestyle changes
