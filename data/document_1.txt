# Healthcare Guide: Common Medical Conditions

## Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

### Overview
Hypertension is a condition where blood pressure is 130/80 mm Hg or higher on multiple readings. It's often called the "silent killer" because many people have it without knowing.

### Risk Factors
- Age (risk increases with age)
- Family history of hypertension
- Obesity or being overweight
- Lack of physical activity
- High sodium diet
- Excessive alcohol consumption
- Stress
- Smoking

### Symptoms
Many people with hypertension have no symptoms. When symptoms occur, they may include:
- Headaches (especially back of head)
- Shortness of breath
- Nosebleeds
- Chest discomfort
- Vision problems
- Blood in urine

### Diagnosis
Blood pressure is measured in millimeters of mercury (mm Hg) with two numbers:
- Systolic pressure (top number): pressure when heart beats
- Diastolic pressure (bottom number): pressure when heart rests
- Normal: less than 120/80 mm Hg
- Elevated: 120-129/<80 mm Hg
- Stage 1 hypertension: 130-139/80-89 mm Hg
- Stage 2 hypertension: 140 or higher/90 or higher mm Hg

### Treatment Options
1. Lifestyle Modifications:
   - Reduce sodium intake to less than 2,300 mg per day
   - Maintain healthy weight (BMI 18.5-24.9)
   - Exercise 150 minutes per week at moderate intensity
   - Limit alcohol consumption
   - Manage stress through meditation or yoga
   - Quit smoking
   - Increase potassium-rich foods

2. Medications:
   - ACE inhibitors (e.g., lisinopril, enalapril)
   - Angiotensin II receptor blockers (ARBs)
   - Beta-blockers
   - Calcium channel blockers
   - Diuretics
   - Combination medications

### Complications
- Heart disease
- Stroke
- Kidney disease
- Vision loss
- Metabolic syndrome
- Heart failure

### Prevention
- Maintain healthy weight
- Exercise regularly
- Eat heart-healthy diet
- Limit sodium
- Reduce stress
- Limit alcohol
- Monitor blood pressure regularly


## Type 2 Diabetes

### Overview
Type 2 diabetes is a chronic condition where the body cannot effectively use insulin (insulin resistance) or doesn't produce enough insulin. It accounts for 90-95% of all diabetes cases.

### Risk Factors
- Obesity or being overweight
- Age 45 or older
- Family history of diabetes
- Physical inactivity
- Prediabetes
- History of gestational diabetes
- Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
- Certain ethnic backgrounds (Hispanic, African American, Native American, Asian American, Pacific Islander)

### Symptoms
- Increased thirst
- Frequent urination (especially at night)
- Increased hunger
- Fatigue or tiredness
- Blurred vision
- Cuts or sores that heal slowly
- Tingling or numbness in hands or feet
- Unexplained weight loss

### Diagnosis
- Fasting blood glucose test: 126 mg/dL or higher = diabetes
- Oral glucose tolerance test: 200 mg/dL or higher = diabetes
- Random blood glucose test: 200 mg/dL or higher with symptoms = diabetes
- Hemoglobin A1C (HbA1C) test: 6.5% or higher = diabetes

### Treatment Options
1. Lifestyle Management:
   - Weight loss (5-10% of body weight)
   - Balanced diet with whole grains, lean proteins, fruits, vegetables
   - Regular physical activity (150 minutes per week)
   - Monitor blood glucose levels
   - Manage stress
   - Get adequate sleep

2. Medications:
   - Metformin (first-line medication)
   - Sulfonylureas
   - Meglitinides
   - DPP-4 inhibitors
   - GLP-1 receptor agonists
   - SGLT2 inhibitors
   - Thiazolidinediones
   - Insulin therapy (in advanced cases)

### Complications
- Heart disease and stroke
- Kidney disease
- Vision problems and blindness
- Nerve damage (neuropathy)
- Foot problems and amputations
- Dental disease
- Cognitive decline

### Prevention
- Maintain healthy weight
- Exercise regularly
- Eat healthy diet
- Don't smoke
- Monitor blood glucose if prediabetic


## Heart Disease

### Overview
Heart disease encompasses various conditions affecting the heart, including coronary artery disease, heart attack, heart failure, and arrhythmias.

### Risk Factors
- Age (men 45+, women 55+)
- Gender (men at higher risk)
- Family history
- Smoking
- High blood pressure
- High cholesterol
- Diabetes
- Obesity
- Physical inactivity
- Stress
- Poor diet

### Symptoms
- Chest pain or pressure
- Shortness of breath
- Pain in arm, jaw, neck, or back
- Indigestion or heartburn
- Palpitations
- Weakness or fatigue
- Dizziness or lightheadedness

### Diagnosis
- Physical examination and medical history
- Electrocardiogram (EKG)
- Stress test
- Coronary angiography
- CT or MRI imaging
- Echocardiogram
- Blood tests

### Treatment Options
1. Lifestyle Changes:
   - Heart-healthy diet (Mediterranean diet)
   - Regular exercise
   - Weight management
   - Stress management
   - Smoking cessation
   - Limit alcohol

2. Medications:
   - Aspirin
   - Beta-blockers
   - ACE inhibitors
   - Statins
   - Nitrates
   - Anticoagulants

3. Procedures:
   - Angioplasty and stent placement
   - Bypass surgery
   - Pacemaker implantation
   - Heart transplant (in severe cases)
